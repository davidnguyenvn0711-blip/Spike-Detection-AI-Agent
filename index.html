The business problem is timing.

The current spike-order report identifies abnormal customer demand only 1–3 days before shipment. At that point, production schedules, inventory allocations, and customer commitments are already difficult to change.

Current State

Reactive spike reporting

Managers discover abnormal orders too late, then scramble to confirm customer intent, check inventory, and decide whether shipment should proceed.

Future State

AI-driven early warning

The system flags suspicious demand patterns earlier and gives managers a clear explanation before release decisions are made.

Why this matters.

Spike orders create operational risk across inventory, production, sales, and customer service.

Inventory

Large single orders can consume a major share of available stock and reduce buffer for other customers.

Production

Unexpected demand can force schedule changes, expedited work, and poor allocation decisions.

Customer Service

Late visibility makes it harder to explain availability, lead time, or shipment decisions.

Customer Satisfaction

Stockouts and shipment holds can damage trust when communication happens too late.

Revenue

Wrong decisions can create lost sales, rushed fulfillment cost, or unfair stock allocation.

Supply Continuity

Potential competitive stockpiling can drain inventory and weaken service levels for regular demand.

3. Why These Orders Were Flagged

These orders show high forecast consumption, large inventory exposure, abnormal customer ordering behavior, and possible stockpiling signals. Some orders approach or exceed a full month of forecasted demand while also consuming a large share of available stock.

4. Recommended Manager Action

Require planner review on all flagged bookings before shipment release. Place short holds on the strongest signals until customer use, PO authenticity, rush reason, and inventory impact are verified.

Verification Checklist

  • Confirm PO authenticity and project reference.
  • Verify final ship-to location and end-use.
  • Validate rush shipment reason.
  • Check customer historical behavior and abnormal multiplier.
  • Consider staged shipment release if exposure is high.
  • Document manager decision in the decision log.

Human-in-the-loop design.

The AI does not make the final operating decision. It flags risk, explains why, and supports manager judgment.

Step 1

AI Recommendation

The workflow identifies risky orders and explains the signal.

Step 2

Manager Review

A human validates customer intent, PO authenticity, rush reason, and inventory impact.

Step 3

Final Decision

The manager approves, reviews, holds, or escalates before shipment release.
